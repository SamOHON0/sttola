Sustainability

Farming in step with the Burren.

Sustainable farming means producing food without harming the land, the water or the wildlife it depends on.

It protects biodiversity by keeping good habitats for plants, animals and wildlife, and it encourages people to buy their food locally and in season. It is more expensive and more labour-intensive than conventional farming, but the methods work with natural systems rather than against them, and they protect and enhance the environment for the future.

We are proud founding members of the Burren Ecotourism Network and members of Bord Bia's Origin Green programme. Both hold strict standards for green food production, farming and tourism, with full training and ongoing learning required to stay in membership.

The herd & the land

What it means to St Tola.

Our herd of Saanen, Toggenburg and British Alpine goats is reared on 65 acres of unspoiled pasture, some of the finest farmland of its type in Europe. In summer they graze herb-rich fields of buttercup, meadowsweet and wild garlic. Through winter they are housed in clean, spacious sheds and fed hay grown on the farm and non-GMO grain.

Every kid is hand-reared after a long weaning on its mother's milk. Strict standards for housing and hygiene keep the herd happy and healthy, and that care is what you taste in the cheese.

Our policy

The Geopark Code of Practice, lived day to day.

We are fully committed to the Burren and Cliffs of Moher Geopark Sustainable Code of Practice for Tourism, and to strengthening our environmental, social, cultural and economic sustainability across the whole business.

People & place

Working together

We help develop the Burren as a sustainable destination, attending training and networking through the year and taking part in the Geopark Marketing Group and the Burren Food Trail.

A cared-for landscape

Natural, on-site fertilisers only. We've planted 20 apple trees for bees and half an acre of willow to offset carbon and aid drainage, put up bird boxes, leave wild uncut margins, take part in beach clean-ups and follow Leave No Trace across the business.

A well-understood heritage

Our interpretive centre shares the story of the Burren and Cliffs of Moher Geopark. Staff are trained in the landscape and heritage of the area, with books, maps and leaflets on hand for visitors.

Vibrant communities

We host regular public tours and open days that bring education on farming and conservation, so tourism gives back to the community as well as our guests.

Strengthened livelihoods

70% of our purchases are from local suppliers. We employ 6 people off-season and up to 8 in peak season and at Christmas, support local fundraising, and promote the region to every visitor.

Resource efficiency

Energy

49 solar panels, running since January 2025, now produce roughly half our electricity, and solar also heats some of our water. Add insulation, energy-efficient lighting and regular audits. Target: 80% of our energy produced on site by 2027.

Water

Monthly water monitoring against production, flow restrictors where appropriate, reduced-flush WCs, and rainwater harvesting for farm wash-down.

Waste

Farming and production by-products are treated on site in a custom aeration tank, overseen by the University of Limerick. Paper, glass, card, plastic and metal are recycled, organics composted, and compostable towels used throughout.

Sustainable transport

70% of staff walk or cycle to work, the rest car-pool where they can. Local deliveries run on set days, and visitors who arrive by bike get 10% off.

Green purchasing

90% of our inputs are produced on site, starting with our own milk, and 85% of fodder is grown here. Packaging is moving to recycled and biodegradable; 28% of purchases are eco, organic or recycled, targeting 35% by 2026.

Managed and reviewed

We produce an annual environmental plan with clear objectives, targets and actions; comply fully with relevant legislation; monitor and record our environmental impact; and openly share our policies with customers, suppliers and contractors. Reduce, reuse, recycle runs through everything we do.

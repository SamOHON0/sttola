Our people

Meet our team.

The cheese is made by a small, dedicated team in Gortbofearna. A few of the people behind it:

Director, Sales & Marketing

Siobhán Ní Gháirbhith

A former school teacher turned cheesemaker. Siobhán took over St Tola when the original owners retired in 1999. She is a former Chairperson of CAIS, a member of Slow Food Ireland and the Taste Council, a member of the National Council for ISME and current chairperson of Irish Cheese Direct. A fervent believer in sustainable farming, a fluent Irish speaker and a passionate advocate of Irish food, culture and music, Siobhán looks after sales and marketing for St Tola.

Director

John Harrington

John is Siobhán's partner in life and in business. A former school teacher turned entrepreneur, he is now a managing director of Inagh Farmhouse Cheese and the financial controller behind the business. His excellent problem-solving skills make him an invaluable member of the team.

Production Manager

Carmen Gal

Carmen is responsible for all aspects of the production operation and ensures it is run in accordance with the Code of Practice. With Inagh Farmhouse Cheese since 2003, she has completed HACCP training and numerous cheesemaking courses. Her attention to detail and natural skill in tasting ensure St Tola is produced to the highest quality.

Production Manager

Brian McEntee

Brian has worked for Inagh Farmhouse Cheese since March 2008. He holds a diploma in Business Studies and Hotel & Catering Management from DIT and spent many years in food service, bringing a broad knowledge of food safety and hygiene, organic gardening and a passion for good food. The ultimate multi-tasker, he can be found turning his hand to any job on the farm or in production.

Farm Manager

Petru Gal

Petru has been with Inagh Farmhouse Cheese since 2003 and manages and cares for the 280-strong herd of milking goats. A skilled herdsman and manager, his love of the land and animals ensures the herd is very well cared for, and produces delicious milk.

Accounts

Clive Wagenaar

A relatively new member of the team, Clive hails from South Africa and now looks after accounts at St Tola.

Farm & Cheesehouse

Mihaly Ferencz

Mihaly has worked at St Tola since 2019. Originally from Romania and with a background in construction, he is a versatile and solid member of our team, both on the farm and in the cheesehouse.

Farm Hand

Shane Griffin

Shane is our local lad on the farm: a quiet, gentle giant and a great help around the animals.

Farm Tours

Gráinne Casey

Gráinne has worked for Inagh Farmhouse Cheese since 2004. After many years in sales and marketing in England, she brings that energy to promoting our cheese and telling our story to the world, and looks after farm tours and visits.

Our student workforce: St Tola welcomes French students every year from February to October, from ESA College in Angers and Purpan INP in Toulouse.
